A book about math and memory - 201 words

The Housekeeper and the Professor (2003), by Yoko Ogawa, tells a story about a housekeeper. She is hired to care for a smart mathematician. His memory only lasts 80 minutes because of an accident. Every day, he forgets and rediscovers the world and people around him. But he creates special connections through his love of numbers. The narrator shows patience, empathy and never gives up. When facing the Professor's problems,

she builds a relationship based on acceptance and understanding. Her son, who the Professor calls Root, joins them. The woman becomes the emotional support, creating human connection every day even though he forgets. Ogawa explores the beauty of temporary moments and small things. The novel asks what remains when memory fades: humanity, kindness and shared feelings. Math becomes a common language showing the hidden beauty of the world. The relationship between these three characters celebrates the human ability to create meaning and warmth.

This novel illustrates pair memorisation well with this excerpt: “Whenever I saw prime numbers, I thought of the Professor. Numbers hid everywhere in everyday life — on a supermarket receipt, a doorplate, a bus schedule, a use-by date on a pack of ham, a note from Root on a test… While remaining faithful to their official role, they bravely protected their original meaning, hidden behind it.”
